===== PAGE 21 =====
Personalized Tutoring
•
Benjamin Bloom’s Two Sigma Problem [2]
•
Gap between 1-on-1 tutoring and classroom = 2 sigma
•
AI makes personalized tutoring possible
•
Instant, personalized (memory feature), and adjusting
•
Tips
•
Help AI understand your situation (motivation, level of understanding)
•
Allow AI to interview you
•
e.g., "I would like to build a personal website with GitHub. Please interview me first to better 
understand my level of understanding and coach me step by step."
[1] Mollick, "Co-Intelligence"
[2] Bloom (1984). https://web.mit.edu/5.95/readings/bloom-two-sigma.pdf

===== PAGE 26 =====
Hallucination
•
AI makes plausible but entirely fake facts
•
Why? Incentives drive guessing [1]
•
What is a good task taker’s strategy?
•
Recent research: zero hallucination → measurable reliability [2]
•
More discussion after we understand how LLMs are built
•
Solutions?
•
Tons of research, but model improvements are faster
•
CoT, CoV, Source-Grounding, Output constraints, uncertainty acknowledgement…
•
Three personal takes:
•
Multi-model cross validation
•
Limit the source (e.g., Notebook LM)
•
The bottom line is human-in-the-loop
[1] https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.04664
[2] https://www.lakera.ai/blog/guide-to-hallucinations-in-large-language-models
