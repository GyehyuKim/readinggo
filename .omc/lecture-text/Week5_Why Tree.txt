===== PAGE 2 =====
Why Tree is a method developed with the inspiration of Work Domain Analysis 
(WDA) that Rasmussen originally developed. I found that means-ends link is a 
powerful tool to understand and design a system. In some senses, the overall 
structure of Why Tree is a scaled down version of WDA, but the means-ends links 
are more nuanced to help people understand how to apply the technique in 
everyday problem solving and design.

===== PAGE 8 =====
FAQ
●
What are the benefits of Why Tree?
○
By "Why Up," you think deeper on the essence of what you are doing.
■
Sometimes, intermediate whys converge into a single why.
■
That's often a core value of the system that you deal with.
○
By "How Down," you can quickly enumerate other ways to serve the why.
■
This slightly differs from random brainstorming. 
1.
In Why Tree, you can have more focused brainstorming.
2.
In Why Tree, you may have multiple layers of whys.
○
By alternating these two, you can deepen your thinking.
■
Often, the resulting Why Tree reveal the overall structure of a system.
■
Some hows are intuitive instances of your underlying whys. 
1.
Yes, the ultimate why will be your core desire, but hows help you realize it.
